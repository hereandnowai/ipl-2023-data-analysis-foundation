# Section 6: PROJECT-SPECIFIC CONCEPTS - Monte Carlo Simulation Logic

A **Monte Carlo Simulation** uses randomness to solve problems that might be deterministic in principle.

### The Math: Probability & Randomness
If we want to estimate $\pi$, we can throw "darts" (random $(x, y)$ points) at a square. The ratio of darts that fall inside a circle divided by the total tells us about $\pi$.

$$\pi \approx 4 \times \frac{\text{hits}}{\text{total}}$$

### Real-World Analogy
Testing if a new bridge will hold is impossible in reality without a bridge. A Monte Carlo Simulation is like running 10,000 "virtual bridge tests" with slightly different wind and load conditions to see how many "fail."
